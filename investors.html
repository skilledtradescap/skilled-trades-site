For prospective investors

Deal-by-deal exposure to Maryland’s skilled-trade economy.

We originate and structure revenue-based financing for established HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing and home-service businesses in Maryland, offering investors exposure to a niche, illiquid, higher-risk private credit strategy.

Investment thesis

Skilled-trade businesses sit at the intersection of durable demand and constrained supply. A combination of aging infrastructure, housing stock, electrification, and demographic trends supports long-term demand for local services. Yet many profitable businesses remain under-financed due to size, collateral, or documentation gaps.

Segment focus

HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, and home services in Maryland with 2–3+ years of operating history, positive or near-positive cash flow, and clear, executable growth plans.

Capital use

Funding is directed to hiring additional technicians, adding vehicles, purchasing equipment, expanding into new territories, and upgrading operational systems rather than refinancing legacy debt.

Opportunity

This is a niche that is often too small for private equity and too unconventional for banks, despite stable underlying economics and strong local relevance.

Structure and return profile

Instrument

Revenue-based financing: investors receive a share of monthly revenue (e.g. 5–8%) until a pre-agreed multiple of invested capital (e.g. 1.3–1.6x) is repaid.

Deal-by-deal SPVs

Capital is deployed through special purpose vehicles (SPVs) on a transaction-by-transaction basis. Investors can choose which transactions to participate in rather than committing to a blind pool.

Target duration

Depending on the business and revenue trajectory, the target repayment horizon is typically 18–36 months. Actual outcomes can be shorter or longer.

Any reference to returns is illustrative only and does not represent a guarantee or prediction of future performance.

Risk, alignment, and suitability

This is an inherently higher-risk, illiquid strategy focused on small businesses. Underwriting is conservative, but risks include business underperformance, concentration, economic shocks, and changes in demand or regulation.

Alignment

Repayment is tied to revenue, aligning incentives between owners and investors. Capital is structured to finance growth, not to rescue distressed situations.

Underwriting focus

Attention is placed on track record, revenue quality, margin structure, owner capability, and specific growth use-of-funds rather than just collateral coverage.

Suitability

This approach is not suitable for all investors. Any future offering would be limited to appropriately qualified investors in compliance with applicable regulations.
